Summary Statistics

- The most common crime in Washington D.C. is theft at 38.59% of overall reports. - Less than 2% of all crime takes place during extreme temperatures meaning anytime above 90°F or below 32F - 12.62% of crime happens in the morning, 26.89% in the afternoon, 22.22% in the evening, and lastly 38.27% at night. - After running a linear regression test we found a p-value of 0.0012 and an r-value of 0.355 suggesting a moderately positive correlation between temperature and crime.
